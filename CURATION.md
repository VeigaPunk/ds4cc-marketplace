# DS4CC curation policy

DS4CC is a living marketplace, but not an indiscriminate catalog. We keep the
strongest entries in each domain we explore and update the reviewed set as
better evidence or better tools emerge.

## Admission gate

An entry must have:

1. A clear, useful capability with a reproducible demonstration.
2. Source provenance and an identifiable maintainer or publisher.
3. Current documentation, installation steps, and supported-platform scope.
4. Compatible licensing and required notices.
5. A reviewed security and privacy posture appropriate to its permissions.
6. Automated structural checks plus a manual end-to-end capability check.
7. Claims limited to what the evidence demonstrates.

Entries can be removed or demoted when they become unmaintained, unsafe,
misleading, legally unclear, or dominated by a demonstrably stronger option.

## Claim discipline

Comparative, compatibility, price, and “full functionality” claims require a
dated evidence packet. The packet must identify tested hardware and software,
the comparison baseline, known limitations, and a repeatable demonstration.

### Proposed DS4CC microG positioning

The intended claim is:

> DS4CC microG delivers Gmicro-equivalent functionality using a spare
> DualSense or DualShock controller over USB or Bluetooth, at no charge
> instead of purchasing the approximately US$220 device.

This wording is approved for a public listing only after the repository
contains:

- a Gmicro feature-parity matrix;
- USB and Bluetooth demonstrations using both controller families;
- tested operating-system, firmware, and device versions;
- setup and troubleshooting documentation;
- known limitations and latency measurements;
- source and license provenance; and
- a dated source for the approximately US$220 comparison price.

Until those artifacts exist, DS4CC microG remains a proposed marketplace entry,
not a capability advertised by the published DS4CC Marketplace app.
